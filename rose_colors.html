Rose Colors

  1. Red Roses

    2. Symbolism: Red roses are the ultimate symbol of love and passion. They are often used to convey deep emotions, romantic gestures, and admiration.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Mr. Lincoln: A classic hybrid tea rose, known for its deep red color and strong fragrance. It has large, elegant blooms and is highly regarded for its long stems.
      • Chrysler Imperial: Known for its velvety, dark red blooms, this hybrid tea rose is often praised for its stunning appearance and strong scent.


      • Red Gold: A combination of red and gold, this rose offers a beautiful color contrast and has a pleasing fragrance.
      • Red Eden: This climbing rose is popular for its lush, full blooms and is often used in gardens and trellises.
      • Black Baccara: This unique rose has a dark, almost black-red hue. It's known for its romantic appearance and is often used in arrangements for special occasions.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged.
      • Sunlight: Full sun is ideal for optimal blooming.
      • Fertilizing: Use a balanced rose fertilizer during the growing season.

  2. White Roses

    3. Symbolism: White roses represent purity, innocence, and new beginnings. They are often used in weddings and formal events.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Margaret Merril: Known for its elegant white blooms and strong fragrance, this hybrid tea rose is both beautiful and resilient.
      • Sterling Silver: This hybrid tea rose has a light silvery hue with a hint of lavender. While not purely white, its soft color makes it a favorite for arrangements.
      • White Dawn: A climbing rose with large, fragrant white blooms, White Dawn is perfect for trellises or fences and blooms repeatedly throughout the season.
      • Alba Rose: Also known as the "White Rose of York," this historic variety has delicate, cup-shaped blooms and a lovely fragrance. It's a great choice for gardens with a classic feel.
      • Pope John Paul II: This hybrid tea rose features large, creamy white blooms with a subtle fragrance. It's known for its vigorous growth and disease resistance.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a rose-specific fertilizer to promote strong growth.

  3. Black Roses

    4. Symbolism: Black roses often symbolize death, mourning, or the end of something, like a relationship. They can also represent rebellion or the idea of new beginnings emerging from loss. In some contexts, black roses are associated with the macabre or gothic themes, but they can also carry a sense of mystery or the unknown. Their meaning can vary depending on the context and the intent behind giving or displaying them.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Burgundy Iceberg: A dark variation of the classic Iceberg rose, this variety has deep burgundy blooms that can appear almost black, especially in the evening light.
      • Black Magic: This rose has deep, velvety blooms that are rich red to near-black, making it a favorite for arrangements and special occasions.
      • Midnight Blue: A dark purple rose that gives an impression of black, known for its stunning color and unique appearance.
      • Velvet Night: A dark red rose that can look black in certain lighting, appreciated for its lush petals and classic shape.
      • Ebb Tide: This rose features deep purple blooms with a rich fragrance, often considered a favorite among dark-colored roses.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a rose-specific fertilizer to promote strong growth.

  4. Pink Roses

    5. Symbolism: Pink roses signify admiration, gratitude, and elegance. They are versatile and can be used for various occasions from birthdays to romantic gestures.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Queen Elizabeth: A classic hybrid tea rose, known for its large, elegant pink blooms and strong fragrance. It has a long blooming period and sturdy stems.
      • Pink Eden: A climbing rose with soft pink blooms, this variety is known for its lush foliage and fragrant flowers. It's great for trellises and fences.
      • Sweet Julia: This rose features delicate, light pink blooms and a sweet fragrance, making it a favorite for gardens and floral arrangements.
      • Simone: A hybrid tea rose with large, elegant blooms in a rich, deep pink. It is known for its beauty and fragrance.
      • Knock Out: This popular landscape rose features vibrant pink flowers that bloom repeatedly throughout the season. It's low-maintenance and disease-resistant.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Keep soil consistently moist.
      • Sunlight: Full sun to ensure healthy growth.
      • Fertilizing: Feed with a balanced fertilizer.

  5. Yellow Roses

    6. Symbolism: Yellow roses are associated with friendship, joy, and new beginnings. They are perfect for celebrating achievements and brightening someone's day.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Sunrise: This hybrid tea rose features bright yellow blooms with a hint of orange at the edges. It has a lovely fragrance and strong stems, making it great for cutting.
      • Golden Showers: A climbing rose with vibrant yellow blooms that have a light, fruity fragrance. It's ideal for trellises and fences.
      • Midas Touch: Known for its rich, golden-yellow blooms, this hybrid tea rose has a classic shape and strong fragrance, making it a favorite for gardens.
      • Chrysler Imperial: Although typically recognized for its dark red color, some cultivars have stunning yellow blooms that provide a striking contrast in the garden.
      • Julia Child: This floribunda rose has soft, buttery yellow blooms with a lovely scent. It's known for its disease resistance and continuous blooming.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Water regularly, but avoid letting the plant sit in water.
      • Sunlight: Enjoys full sun for vibrant blooms.
      • Fertilizing: Use a rose fertilizer to maintain health and flowering.

  6. Green Roses

    7. Green roses symbolize fertility, growth, and renewal. They are often associated with nature and life, representing balance and harmony. In some contexts, green roses can also signify hope, optimism, and new beginnings. Because of their unique color, they can convey a sense of freshness and vitality, making them a great choice for celebrations or as gifts for new ventures.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Green Ice: This variety features delicate, pale green blooms with a slight tint of white.
      • Viridiflora: Known for its unique green color, this rose has small, cupped blooms that are sometimes mixed with other colors.
      • Lime Green: This variety showcases bright lime-green petals that are striking and modern, making them a favorite for contemporary floral designs.
      • Enchantress: This variety features a beautiful, light green hue with a hint of cream, making it a favorite for elegant arrangements.
      • Green Tea Rose: A soft, pale green rose that has a delicate appearance and a lovely fragrance. It's often used in wedding bouquets.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a rose-specific fertilizer to promote strong growth.

  7. Orange Roses

    8. Symbolism: Orange roses convey enthusiasm, passion, and admiration. They are a vibrant choice for expressing excitement and energy.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Tropicana: This hybrid tea rose is known for its striking, bright orange blooms and fruity fragrance. It's a classic favorite and a standout in gardens.
      • Amber Sun: A beautiful floribunda rose with soft, apricot-orange petals. It has a lovely fragrance and is great for cutting.
      • Marmalade Skies: This variety features a stunning mix of orange and yellow hues, creating a warm, sunset-like effect. It's known for its strong blooms and fragrance.
      • Viva Las Vegas: A bold and bright orange rose that is both hardy and disease-resistant. It's perfect for adding a pop of color to any garden.
      • Lady Emma Hamilton: A David Austin rose, this variety showcases rich, warm orange blooms with a classic shape and strong fragrance. It's perfect for romantic gardens.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Keep soil evenly moist and well-drained.
      • Sunlight: Requires full sun to thrive.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a balanced rose fertilizer for best results.

  8. Blue Roses

    9. Blue roses symbolize mystery and the unattainable, representing things that are elusive, as well as enchantment and magic, reflecting romantic ideals; they also signify unique love and spiritual growth, embodying the quest for deeper meaning.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Blue Moon: A hybrid tea rose known for its stunning, lavender-blue blooms and strong fragrance.
      • Blue Girl: This variety features soft, silvery-blue petals and a classic rose shape, often used in arrangements for its beauty.
      • Rhapsody in Blue: A floribunda rose with vibrant blue-purple flowers that have a lovely scent, perfect for gardens.
      • Blue Paradise: This variety offers a stunning light blue color and is often appreciated for its vigorous growth and disease resistance.
      • Moody Blues: A unique variety with deep blue and purple hues, making it a striking addition to floral arrangements.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun to partial shade.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a rose-specific fertilizer to promote strong growth.

  9. Lavender Roses

    10. Symbolism: Lavender roses represent enchantment, grace, and admiration. They are often used to convey a sense of charm and beauty.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Blue Moon: Known for its soft lavender-blue petals, this hybrid tea rose has a delightful fragrance and elegant blooms.
      • Lavender Dream: This variety features beautiful, fragrant blooms in a lovely lavender shade. It's often used in gardens and arrangements for its unique color.
      • Perfume Delight: This rose has a lovely lavender hue and is celebrated for its exceptional fragrance, making it a favorite for gardens and bouquets.
      • Angel Face: This hybrid tea rose boasts a lovely blend of lavender and peach colors, with a delightful scent that makes it popular among rose lovers.
      • Sterling Silver: A classic hybrid tea rose, it has a silvery-lavender hue with a beautiful, strong scent. It's a favorite among gardeners for its stunning appearance.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Ensure the soil is well-drained and consistently moist.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun for optimal growth.
      • Fertilizing: Use a rose-specific fertilizer to support flowering.

  10. Multi-Colored Roses

    11. Symbolism: Multi-colored roses combine the meanings of different colors, making them unique and versatile for various occasions.

    • Popular Varieties:
      • Rainbow Sorbet: This floribunda rose features a beautiful mix of yellow, pink, and orange, creating a cheerful and eye-catching bloom.
      • Psychedelic: This rose has a lively combination of colors, including pink, yellow, and orange, creating a fun and vibrant appearance.
      • Cinderella: A charming floribunda rose with petals that transition from soft pink to cream, often with hints of yellow in the center.
      • Cotillion: This rose has petals that range from pale pink to creamy white, often with hints of peach, providing a soft, multi-colored effect.
      • Ketchup and Mustard: This unique variety features a striking combination of red and yellow petals, making it a bold choice for any garden.
    • Care Tips:
      • Watering: Water regularly, keeping the soil evenly moist.
      • Sunlight: Prefers full sun for vibrant blooms.
      • Fertilizing: Apply a balanced fertilizer to maintain health.
